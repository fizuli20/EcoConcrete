The Problem

Cement Is Essential — And It's Killing the Planet

The construction industry underpins modern civilization, but cement production is one of the world's largest industrial CO₂ sources. Every ton of cement manufactured releases up to 0.9 tons of CO₂ into the atmosphere.

We can't stop building. But we can transform what building means for the climate. Governments are tightening carbon regulations. ESG pressure is mounting. The industry needs a solution that works at scale, at cost, and without operational disruption.

The Technology

Chemistry That Captures

Our proprietary nano-mineral additive accelerates natural carbonation inside concrete, permanently mineralizing CO₂ as stable calcium carbonate — the same compound as limestone.

CO₂ + CaO ⟶ CaCO₃
Carbon Dioxide + Calcium Oxide → Calcium Carbonate (Limestone) — Permanently stored inside the concrete matrix
01
🧪

Additive Mixed In

Nano-mineral particles are blended into standard concrete mix — no special equipment required.

02
⚗️

CO₂ Reacts During Curing

As concrete sets, atmospheric CO₂ bonds with calcium compounds, accelerated by our nano-catalysts.

03
🔬

CaCO₃ Precipitates

Stable calcium carbonate crystals fill the microstructure, improving density and compressive strength.

04
♾️

Permanent Carbon Lock

CO₂ is mineralized for 100+ years — not captured temporarily, but structurally embedded.
